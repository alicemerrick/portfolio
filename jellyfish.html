Jellyfish

A tool for anxiety management

mobile application for smartphone + smartwatch
Overview

Jellyfish is a smartwatch + smartphone application that incorporates elements of gamification, and takes advantage of the immediacy of mobile-device interaction, in order to encourage greater self-awareness and positive coping mechanisms for those struggling with anxiety.

Problem

Anxiety disorders are the most common mental illness in the US. Not everyone seeks professional help. Mental health apps have become an increasingly popular means of self-help, but most do not utilize principles from clinically proven anxiety treatments, tailor to individual needs, or promote an understanding of the causal relationship between actions and emotions.

Ideation

We generated a wide variety of concepts for coping with anxiety, and narrowed them down based on their viability, feasibility, and whether they could be adapted to work with elements of gamification.

Journaling

Jornaling is a cornerstone of Cognitive-Behavioral therapy. With mobile technology, people can journal their thoughts in a mobile app journal wherever they are. Journaling thoughts and feelings allows people with anxiety to conceptualize their anxiety in words. It also allows those with anxiety to find patterns with their anxiety, giving them a sense of control over their anxiety.

E-mote

A person can tap a wearable device that will record the time, place and possibly even photographic data when their anxiety is being triggered. By tracking the time and location of anxiety, people can later review what happened in those situations or analyze larger patterns in their anxiety data.

Positive thinking

People can journal their positive thoughts, interactions, and events in a mobile app journal. This journaling can help track what makes a person happy and remind the individual of happy events that have occurred.

Storyboarding

As part of the refinement process, we developed a story of how the product could be used. It begins with a wearable device. A person suffering from a panic attack or other form of emotional distress may not have the time or ability to journal their thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. The wearable device allows them to capture data that they can reflect upon later.
