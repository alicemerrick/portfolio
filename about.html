About

I used to work as a research assistant in Los Angeles where I designed and built screen-based experiments for testing computational models human cognition. I often found that the subtleties of my experiment's interface could alter participants' responses. That was a frustrating realization for someone trying to test a mathematical model, but it was also how I discovered the field of interaction design.

Now I am residing in Seattle, and completing a master's degree in human-computer interaction at University of Washington. I am interested in information visualization, education, inclusive design, and tool development.
